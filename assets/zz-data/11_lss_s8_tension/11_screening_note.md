# Screening note

Bounded screening run with `|q0_eff| <= 1e-02`.
Resulting screened value: `S8 = 0.828478` for target `S8 = 0.770`.
Contrast gain used: `2.409`.
Today, the effective coupling is `q0eff_LSS(a=1) = -1.000000e-02` and `q0eff_GW(a=1) = -1.000000e-06`.

Assessment:
- Inference: this prototype is not directly compatible with standard chameleon screening, because the transition is imposed in scale factor and wavenumber rather than emerging from a density-dependent effective mass.
- Inference: it is also not a standard Vainshtein realization, because the suppression is not generated by nonlinear derivative interactions around sources.
- The bounded run remains close to the GW-safe branch but misses the full S8 target, which indicates that a classical screening picture would need additional dynamics beyond this phenomenological kernel.
- A separate density-based toy model (`11_chameleon_test.py`) keeps the local branch at `q0eff \approx -10^{-6}` but still returns `S8 \approx 0.829667`, so a simple $\rho_m(a)$-dependent conformal screening is not sufficient either.
- The scale conflict between GW and LSS therefore appears to require a richer nonlinear mechanism than a single conformal coupling dressed by time, scale, or background density alone.
- A sharp late-trigger transition (`11_late_trigger_solver.py`) improves the suppression only to `S8 \approx 0.813495`, so time modulation by itself is also insufficient.
- By contrast, the explicit scale-dependent split tested in `11_k_dependent_solver.py` gives `q0_LSS \approx -1.999996e-03`, `q0_GW \approx -1.000999e-06`, `S8_LSS \approx 0.772525`, and `S8_GW \approx 0.829970`.
- Inference: within the family of Chapter 11 toy models tested here, an explicit dependence on scale $k$ is the only mechanism that simultaneously preserves the GW-safe branch and reaches the cosmological target.
