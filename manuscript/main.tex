\begin{abstract}
\noindent Metric-Coupled Gravity Theory (\textbf{MCGT}) is investigated in this thesis as an alternative to $\Lambda$CDM capable of addressing the persistent cosmological tensions associated with $H_0$ and $S_8$. The central objective is to determine whether a modified gravitational coupling can remain theoretically stable, compatible with local gravitational-wave constraints, and still alter large-scale structure growth enough to ease weak-lensing discrepancies. The analysis is organized around a dual program: first, the internal consistency of the framework is tested through numerical invariants, stability diagnostics, and controlled parameter scans; second, the phenomenology is confronted with cosmological and multi-messenger observations across local and cosmological scales.

The methodology combines extensive Monte Carlo exploration and targeted dynamical solvers. On the local side, $10^5$ Monte Carlo realizations are used to map the gravitational-wave survival envelope and establish the stringent Chapter 10 constraint $|q_0^*| \le 10^{-6}$. On the cosmological side, linear-growth solvers based on \texttt{solve\_ivp} are used to quantify the modification required to reach the weak-lensing target $S_8 \approx 0.77$, showing that an unscreened cosmological branch needs $|q_0^*| \sim 10^{-3}$. This reveals a scale conflict of roughly a factor of 2000 between the local and large-scale sectors, excluding any universal version of MCGT with a single coupling amplitude.

Several screening mechanisms are then tested systematically, including bounded temporal switching, density-based chameleon-like prescriptions, and late-trigger models. All purely temporal or environmental mechanisms fail: even the most aggressive late activation saturates near $S_8 \approx 0.81$ while remaining LIGO-safe. The decisive result of the thesis is that MCGT survives only when the coupling becomes explicitly scale-dependent. A $k$-dependent transition law separates the local branch, where $|q_0^*| \approx 10^{-6}$, from the cosmological branch, where $q_0^*(k \rightarrow 0) \approx -2 \times 10^{-3}$ yields $S_8 \approx 0.7725$. Among the tested kernels, a Step-Function transition centered at $k_c \approx 10^{-4}\,h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}$ provides the cleanest resolution. The final verdict is therefore unambiguous: universal MCGT is excluded, but a scale-dependent MCGT remains phenomenologically viable and offers a coherent route toward reconciling local and global cosmological tensions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Mathematical Formalism and Key Variables}

\subsection{Action and Effective Lagrangian}
The MCGT framework can be formalized through an effective field theory approach. The generalized action in the Einstein frame is expressed as:
\begin{equation}
    S = \int d^4x \sqrt{-g} \left[ \frac{R}{2\kappa^2} - \frac{1}{2}g^{\mu\nu}\partial_\mu \phi \partial_\nu \phi - V(\phi) \right] + S_m\left[e^{2\beta(\phi)}g_{\mu\nu}, \psi_m\right]
\end{equation}
where $\kappa^2 = 8\pi G$, $R$ is the Ricci scalar, $\phi$ is the driving scalar field, $V(\phi)$ is its potential, and $S_m$ represents the matter action. The crucial feature is the conformal coupling factor $e^{2\beta(\phi)}$, which links the scalar field to the matter sector $\psi_m$. Varying this action with respect to the metric yields the modified Friedmann equations governing the background dynamics.

\subsection{Dynamic Equation of State (CPL)}
To map this scalar-tensor dynamic to observational constraints, the effective dark energy in the $\Psi$TMG realization is modeled phenomenologically by the Chevallier-Polarski-Linder (CPL) parametrization:
\begin{equation}
    w(a) = w_0 + w_a (1 - a) \quad \text{where} \quad a = \frac{1}{1+z}
\end{equation}
The exact best-fit (MAP) values (\textbf{Best-Fit v3.3.0}) are:
$$ w_0 = -0.69, \quad w_a = -2.81 $$
These exact coordinates represent the Maximum A Posteriori (MAP) reference point. The final inference results, however, are reported as marginalized medians with 68\% credible intervals, as detailed in Table~\ref{tab:results} and the corner plot in Figure~\ref{fig:mcmc_corner}.
This configuration transiently crosses the "phantom" divide ($w < -1$), a known feature of certain strongly coupled scalar-tensor theories.

\subsection{Modified Expansion}
The evolution of the expansion rate $H(z)$ is governed by the resulting modified Friedmann equation:
\begin{equation}
    \frac{H^2(z)}{H_0^2} = \Omega_r (1+z)^4 + \Omega_m (1+z)^3 + \Omega_{\Psi\mathrm{TMG}} \exp\left[3 \int_0^z \frac{1+w(z')}{1+z'} dz'\right]
\end{equation}
where we explicitly define $\Omega_{\Psi\mathrm{TMG}} \equiv 1 - \Omega_m - \Omega_r$ at $z=0$ to ensure spatial flatness.
It is important to note that this initial condition matching serves as a standalone theoretical calibration diagnostic, distinct from the primary MCMC cosmological parameter inference which relies on the CMB distance prior.

\subsection*{Chapter 10: Global Likelihood Scan}
\textbf{Focus: Statistical synthesis.}
Combination of probes ($SN + BAO + CMB + RSD$). To robustly sample the posterior distributions, we employ an Affine Invariant Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) ensemble sampler. The analysis utilizes 100 walkers over 10,000 steps per walker, discarding the first 20\% as burn-in phase. Convergence is strictly confirmed by a Gelman-Rubin statistic of $\hat{R} < 1.01$ across all parameters, complemented by stable autocorrelation times and an adequate effective sample size (ESS; specifically, $\mathrm{ESS} > 1450$ for all sampled cosmological parameters), thereby yielding effectively independent samples for robust parameter inference. The overall improvement ($\Delta\chi^2_{total} = -151.6$) indicates a significant likelihood enhancement.

\subsection*{Model Selection and Information Criteria}
We define $\Delta\chi^2 \equiv \chi^2_{\Psi\mathrm{TMG}} - \chi^2_{\Lambda\mathrm{CDM}}$; hence, negative values strictly indicate an improvement over the standard model. To complement the likelihood-level comparison, we evaluate information criteria at the global best-fit (MAP) point. We sample the late-time clustering amplitude through $\sigma_8$ (RMS matter fluctuations at $8 h^{-1}\,\text{Mpc}$, $z=0$), alongside $\Omega_m, H_0, w_0, w_a$, and systematically report $S_8 \equiv \sigma_8 (\Omega_m / 0.3)^{0.5}$ as a derived parameter for direct comparison with weak lensing surveys. For the information criteria evaluation, we define the free parameter count as $k_{\Lambda\mathrm{CDM}} = 3$ ($\Omega_m, H_0, \sigma_8$) and $k_{\Psi\mathrm{TMG}} = 5$ ($\Omega_m, H_0, \sigma_8, w_0, w_a$), accounting for the shared nuisance parameters. Using the full data vector, we compute
\[
\mathrm{AIC} = \chi^2 + 2k, \qquad \mathrm{BIC} = \chi^2 + k \ln n .
\]
Relative to $\Lambda$CDM, we obtain $\Delta \mathrm{AIC} = -147.6$ and $\Delta \mathrm{BIC} = -136.7$. Both values indicate even stronger model-selection support in favor of the $\Psi$TMG baseline, beyond a pure goodness-of-fit effect. The CMB anchor remains tightly controlled with $\chi^2_{\mathrm{CMB}} = 0.04$.
Here, $\chi^2_{\mathrm{CMB}}$ corresponds to the single-constraint residual on the acoustic scale distance prior, rather than the full Planck power spectrum likelihood.
We take $n = 1718$ as the total dimensionality of the combined data vector entering the likelihood ($n = N_{\mathrm{SN}} + N_{\mathrm{CMB}} + N_{\mathrm{BAO}} + N_{\mathrm{RSD}}$), explicitly accounting for the full covariance of the Pantheon+ sample (specifically: $N_{\mathrm{SN}} = 1701$ from Pantheon+, $N_{\mathrm{CMB}} = 1$ from the Planck distance prior, $N_{\mathrm{BAO}} = 6$, and $N_{\mathrm{RSD}} = 10$).

\subsection{Robustness to the Equation of State}
To verify that the tension relief is not an artifact of the baseline CPL ansatz, we repeated the full MCMC inference with two alternative dark-energy parameterizations while keeping the same Pantheon+ + BAO + CMB + RSD likelihood. The first test adopts the Jassal-Bagla-Padmanabhan (JBP) form, which preserves a dynamical $(w_0, w_a)$ sector but redistributes the redshift dependence. The second test imposes a strict constant-$w$ limit, in which $w_a=0$ and the model loses one free degree of freedom. Relative to the optimized $\Lambda$CDM reference, all three $\Psi$TMG realizations remain statistically preferred: CPL yields $\Delta\chi^2 = -87.780$ and $\Delta\mathrm{BIC} = -72.882$, JBP gives $\Delta\chi^2 = -80.646$ and $\Delta\mathrm{BIC} = -65.748$, while the constant-$w$ realization still provides $\Delta\chi^2 = -48.087$ and $\Delta\mathrm{BIC} = -40.638$. Here again, negative values indicate an improvement over the standard model.

Note: To ensure a direct baseline comparison within a standardized computational timeframe, the metrics in this specific robustness subsection were derived from a dedicated set of comparative MCMC runs (3,000 steps). This explains the absolute variance from the global high-resolution values reported in Section 10.

This cross-check is important because it isolates the geometric content of the theory from the detailed choice of $w(a)$. The 2D posteriors in Figure~\ref{fig:eos_robustness} show that the preferred region in the $(\Omega_m, w_0)$ plane remains compact and clearly separated from the $\Lambda$CDM point, even when the functional form of the equation of state is changed. \section{Limitations and Future Work}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Dependence on phenomenological parametrizations:} the present robustness check now covers CPL, JBP, and the constant-$w$ limit, but broader classes of dark-energy histories should still be explored to test the full model dependence of $w(a)$.
    \item \textbf{Perturbation analysis:} the current study is limited to the linear regime ($k \lesssim 1\,h\,\mathrm{Mpc}^{-1}$). Full N-body simulations are required to validate the nonlinear power suppression.
    \item \textbf{Phenomenological nature:} the model is an effective field theory (EFT). A fundamental Lagrangian derivation (micro-physics) constitutes the next theoretical step.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
The Metric-Coupled Gravity Theory (MCGT) provides the theoretical geometric framework, while its specific cosmological implementation, denoted $\Psi$TMG, demonstrates data-level improvements on $H_0$, JWST, and $S_8$. Within the scope of the considered datasets and assumptions, $\Psi$TMG stands as a credible candidate for extending the standard model, subject to the discussed limitations and further validations.

The systematic exploration of the MCGT parameter space, carried out through a campaign of $10^5$ numerical simulations, established rigorous constraints on the coupling between the deviation parameter $q_0^*$ and the spectral index $\alpha$. Adopting a conservative detectability threshold of $0.1\,\mathrm{rad}$ for the phase-dephasing statistic $p_{95}$, we identify a robust upper bound of $|q_0^*| \leq 10^{-6}$ for negative-spectrum regimes ($\alpha < 0$). This analysis shows that the current precision of gravitational-wave detectors excludes more than $70\%$ of the sampled space, restricting MCGT to an extremely narrow stability valley around the General Relativity limit.
